1. Mechanics

1.1 Kinematics

Distance: \( s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)

Velocity: \( v = u + at \)

Acceleration: \( a = \frac{v - u}{t} \)

Displacement: \( s = \frac{(u + v)t}{2} \)

Uniform Acceleration: \( v^2 = u^2 + 2as \)

Relative Velocity: \( v_{AB} = v_A - v_B \)

Projectile Motion Range: \( R = \frac{v^2 \sin 2\theta}{g} \)

Maximum Height of Projectile: \( H = \frac{v^2 \sin^2 \theta}{2g} \)

Time of Flight for Projectile: \( T = \frac{2v \sin \theta}{g} \)

1.2 Dynamics

Newton's First Law: An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force.

Newton's Second Law: \( F = ma \)

Newton's Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Friction Force: \( f = \mu N \)

Work Done: \( W = F \cdot d \cdot \cos \theta \)

Power: \( P = \frac{W}{t} \)

Potential Energy: \( PE = mgh \)

Kinetic Energy: \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \)

Work-Energy Theorem: \( W = \Delta KE \)

Conservation of Energy: \( E_{total} = KE + PE \)

1.3 Work and Energy

Mechanical Work: \( W = F \cdot d \cdot \cos \theta \)

Power: \( P = \frac{W}{t} = F \cdot v \)

Elastic Potential Energy: \( PE_{elastic} = \frac{1}{2} k x^2 \)

Work-Energy Principle: \( W_{net} = \Delta KE \)

Power: \( P = \frac{dW}{dt} = F \cdot v \)

1.4 Rotational Dynamics

Torque: \( \tau = r \times F \)

Moment of Inertia: \( I = \sum m_i r_i^2 \)

Angular Momentum: \( L = I \cdot \omega \)

Angular Kinetic Energy: \( KE_{rot} = \frac{1}{2} I \omega^2 \)

Newton's Law for Rotation: \( \tau = I \cdot \alpha \)

Angular Acceleration: \( \alpha = \frac{\Delta \omega}{t} \)

Rotational Work: \( W_{rot} = \tau \cdot \theta \)

1.5 Gravitation

Universal Law of Gravitation: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \)

Gravitational Potential Energy: \( U = -G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r} \)

Gravitational Field Strength: \( g = \frac{F}{m} = G \frac{M}{r^2} \)

Acceleration Due to Gravity: \( g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2 \text{ (on the surface of Earth)} \)

Gravitational Force on Surface: \( F_g = mg \)

Orbital Velocity: \( v = \sqrt{ \frac{GM}{r} } \)

Escape Velocity: \( v_{esc} = \sqrt{ \frac{2GM}{r} } \)

Kepler's Third Law: \( T^2 \propto r^3 \)

2. Heat and Thermodynamics

2.1 Temperature and Heat

Heat Transfer: \( Q = mc\Delta T \)

Specific Heat Capacity: \( c = \frac{Q}{m \Delta T} \)

Latent Heat: \( Q = mL \)

Latent Heat of Fusion: \( L_f = \frac{Q}{m} \text{ (for melting/freezing)} \)

Latent Heat of Vaporization: \( L_v = \frac{Q}{m} \text{ (for boiling/condensation)} \)

Thermal Expansion (Linear): \( \Delta L = \alpha L_0 \Delta T \)

Thermal Expansion (Volume): \( \Delta V = \beta V_0 \Delta T \)

2.2 Thermodynamic Processes

Work Done by Gas (Isothermal): \( W = nRT \ln \frac{V_f}{V_i} \)

Work Done by Gas (Adiabatic): \( W = \frac{P_1 V_1 - P_2 V_2}{\gamma - 1} \)

First Law of Thermodynamics: \( \Delta U = Q - W \)

Change in Internal Energy: \( \Delta U = n C_V \Delta T \)

Heat Capacity at Constant Volume: \( C_V = \frac{\Delta U}{\Delta T} \)

Heat Capacity at Constant Pressure: \( C_P = \frac{\Delta H}{\Delta T} \)

Enthalpy: \( H = U + pV \)

Adiabatic Process (Relation): \( PV^\gamma = \text{constant} \)

2.3 Laws of Thermodynamics

First Law of Thermodynamics: \( \Delta U = Q - W \)

Second Law of Thermodynamics (Clausius Statement): Heat cannot spontaneously flow from a colder body to a hotter body.

Second Law of Thermodynamics (Kelvin-Planck Statement): It is impossible to devise a cyclic process that converts all absorbed heat into work.

Third Law of Thermodynamics: As temperature approaches absolute zero, the entropy of a system approaches a constant minimum.

Entropy Change: \( \Delta S = \frac{Q}{T} \text{ (for reversible processes)} \)

Carnot Efficiency: \( \eta = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H} \)

Ideal Gas Law: \( PV = nRT \)

Boyle's Law: \( P_1 V_1 = P_2 V_2 \text{ (at constant temperature)} \)

Charles's Law: \( \frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2} \text{ (at constant pressure)} \)

Gay-Lussac's Law: \( \frac{P_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2}{T_2} \text{ (at constant volume)} \)

2.4 Heat Transfer

Conduction: \( Q = \frac{kA \Delta T t}{d} \)

Convection: \( Q = hA \Delta T t \)

Radiation: \( Q = \sigma \epsilon A T^4 t \)

Stefan-Boltzmann Law: \( E = \sigma T^4 \)

3. Geometric and Physical Optics

3.1 Geometric Optics

Lens Maker's Formula: \( \frac{1}{f} = \left( \frac{n - 1}{R_1} - \frac{n - 1}{R_2} \right) \)

Mirror Formula: \( \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v} \)

Magnification (Mirror): \( M = \frac{v}{u} \)

Lens Formula: \( \frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} \)

Magnification (Lens): \( M = \frac{v}{u} \)

Refractive Index: \( n = \frac{c}{v} \)

Snell's Law: \( n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2 \)

Critical Angle: \( \theta_c = \sin^{-1} \left( \frac{n_2}{n_1} \right) \)

Focal Length of a Thin Lens: \( \frac{1}{f} = \left( \frac{n - 1}{R_1} - \frac{n - 1}{R_2} \right) \)

Lens Combination Formula: \( \frac{1}{f_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{f_1} + \frac{1}{f_2} \)

Lens Equation for Multiple Lenses: \( \frac{1}{f_{\text{effective}}} = \frac{1}{f_1} + \frac{1}{f_2} - \frac{d}{f_1 f_2} \)

Angular Magnification: \( M_a = \frac{D}{f_{\text{eye}}} \text{ where } D \text{ is the least distance of distinct vision} \)

3.2 Physical Optics

Young's Double Slit Experiment (Path Difference): \( d \sin \theta = m \lambda \)

Path Difference: \( \Delta x = d \sin \theta \)

Fringe Separation (Young's Experiment): \( \beta = \frac{\lambda D}{d} \)

Diffraction Grating Equation: \( d \sin \theta = m \lambda \)

Rayleigh Criterion (Resolution): \( \theta = 1.22 \frac{\lambda}{D} \)

Huygens' Principle: Each point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets

Interference Condition: \( \Delta \phi = 2n\pi \text{ (constructive interference)}, \Delta \phi = (2n + 1)\pi \text{ (destructive interference)} \)

Polarization (Malus' Law): \( I = I_0 \cos^2 \theta \)

Polarizing Angle (Brewster's Angle): \( \tan \theta_p = \frac{n_2}{n_1} \)

Absorption of Light: \( I = I_0 e^{-\alpha x} \)

Dispersion: \( \text{The separation of light into its constituent colors due to varying speeds in different media} \)

Chromatic Aberration: \( \text{The failure of a lens to focus all colors to the same point} \)

Diffraction Pattern (Single Slit): \( a \sin \theta = m \lambda \)

Angular Width of Central Maximum (Single Slit): \( \Delta \theta = \frac{2 \lambda}{a} \)

Coherence Length: \( L_c = \frac{\lambda^2}{\Delta \lambda} \)

Fraunhofer Diffraction (Single Slit): \( I(\theta) = I_0 \left( \frac{\sin(\beta/2)}{\beta/2} \right)^2 \text{ where } \beta = \frac{2 \pi a \sin \theta}{\lambda} \)

Fraunhofer Diffraction (Double Slit): \( I(\theta) = I_0 \left( \frac{\sin(N\beta/2)}{\sin(\beta/2)} \right)^2 \left( \frac{\sin^2(\alpha/2)}{(\alpha/2)^2} \right) \text{ where } \alpha = \frac{2 \pi d \sin \theta}{\lambda} \text{ and } N \text{ is the number of slits} \)

Optical Path Length (OPL): \( \text{OPL} = n \cdot \text{distance} \)

Optical Coherence: \( \text{The degree to which light waves are correlated in time and space} \)

4. Waves and Sound

4.1 Wave Basics

Wave Speed: \( v = \frac{d}{t} \)

Wave Equation: \( v = f \lambda \)

Frequency: \( f = \frac{1}{T} \)

Wavelength: \( \lambda = \frac{v}{f} \)

Amplitude: The maximum displacement from the equilibrium position

Period: \( T = \frac{1}{f} \)

Wave Number: \( k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \)

Angular Frequency: \( \omega = 2\pi f \)

4.2 Types of Waves

Transverse Wave: Particles move perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation

Longitudinal Wave: Particles move parallel to the direction of wave propagation

Wave Pulse: A single disturbance moving through a medium

Periodic Wave: A wave that repeats at regular intervals

Complex Wave: A wave composed of multiple simple harmonic waves

Traveling Wave: A wave that moves through space or a medium

Standing Wave: A wave that remains in a constant position

4.3 Sound Waves

Speed of Sound in Air: \( v = 331.3 + 0.6 T \text{ (where } T \text{ is temperature in } ^\circ \text{C)} \)

Intensity: \( I = \frac{P}{A} \)

Decibel Level: \( \beta = 10 \log \left(\frac{I}{I_0}\right) \)

Sound Level Change: \( \Delta \beta = 10 \log \left(\frac{I_2}{I_1}\right) \)

Fundamental Frequency: \( f_1 = \frac{v}{2L} \)

Overtones: \( f_n = n \cdot f_1 \text{ (where } n \text{ is the harmonic number)} \)

Beat Frequency: \( f_{\text{beat}} = |f_1 - f_2| \)

Doppler Effect: \( f' = f \left(\frac{v \pm v_o}{v \mp v_s}\right) \)

Sound Intensity and Distance: \( I \propto \frac{1}{r^2} \)

Frequency of a Tuning Fork: \( f = \frac{v}{\lambda} \)

Resonant Frequency of a Tube: \( f_n = \frac{n v}{2L} \text{ (closed tube)} \)

4.4 Interference and Resonance

Constructive Interference: Occurs when \( \Delta \phi = 2n\pi \)

Destructive Interference: Occurs when \( \Delta \phi = (2n + 1)\pi \)

Resonance Condition: \( f_n = \frac{n v}{2L} \)

Standing Wave Pattern: \( \lambda_n = \frac{2L}{n} \)

Nodes and Antinodes: Nodes are points of minimum amplitude; Antinodes are points of maximum amplitude

Superposition Principle: The resultant displacement is the sum of the individual displacements

Interference Pattern (Double Slit): \( y = \frac{m \lambda D}{d} \)

Diffraction Grating: \( d \sin \theta = m \lambda \)

Beat Frequency Formula: \( f_{\text{beat}} = |f_1 - f_2| \)

5. Electricity and Magnetism

5.1 Electrostatics

Coulomb's Law: \( F = k_e \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \)

Electric Field: \( E = \frac{F}{q} = k_e \frac{Q}{r^2} \)

Electric Potential: \( V = \frac{U}{q} = k_e \frac{Q}{r} \)

Potential Difference: \( V = IR \)

Capacitance: \( C = \frac{Q}{V} \)

Energy Stored in a Capacitor: \( U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2 \)

Capacitors in Series: \( \frac{1}{C_{\text{eq}}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{C_n} \)

Capacitors in Parallel: \( C_{\text{eq}} = C_1 + C_2 + \cdots + C_n \)

5.2 Current and Circuits

Ohm's Law: \( V = IR \)

Power in a Circuit: \( P = VI = I^2 R = \frac{V^2}{R} \)

Kirchhoff's Voltage Law: \( \sum V_{\text{around}} = 0 \)

Kirchhoff's Current Law: \( \sum I_{\text{at a junction}} = 0 \)

Series Circuit: \( R_{\text{total}} = R_1 + R_2 + \cdots + R_n \)

Parallel Circuit: \( \frac{1}{R_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{R_n} \)

Resistor-Resistor Combinations: \( R_{\text{total}} = \frac{V}{I} \)

5.3 Electromagnetism

Magnetic Field of a Current-Carrying Wire: \( B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r} \)

Magnetic Force on a Moving Charge: \( F = q v B \sin \theta \)

Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction: \( \mathcal{E} = - \frac{d \Phi_B}{dt} \)

Lenz's Law: The direction of induced current is such that it opposes the change in magnetic flux.

Self-Inductance: \( \mathcal{E} = -L \frac{dI}{dt} \)

Mutual Inductance: \( \mathcal{E}_2 = -M \frac{dI_1}{dt} \)

Magnetic Flux: \( \Phi_B = B \cdot A \cdot \cos \theta \)

Biot-Savart Law: \( \mathbf{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4 \pi} \int \frac{I d\mathbf{l} \times \mathbf{r}}{r^3} \)

5.4 Alternating Current (AC) Circuits

Impedance in an RLC Circuit: \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \)

Reactance of an Inductor: \( X_L = \omega L \)

Reactance of a Capacitor: \( X_C = \frac{1}{\omega C} \)

Resonance Frequency: \( \omega_0 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{LC}} \)

Average Power in AC Circuit: \( P = V_{rms} I_{rms} \cos \phi \)

Power Factor: \( \cos \phi = \frac{R}{Z} \)

Root Mean Square Voltage: \( V_{rms} = \frac{V_{peak}}{\sqrt{2}} \)

Root Mean Square Current: \( I_{rms} = \frac{I_{peak}}{\sqrt{2}} \)

6. Modern Physics

6.1 Quantum Mechanics

Photoelectric Effect: \( E_k = h\nu - \phi \)

de Broglie Wavelength: \( \lambda = \frac{h}{p} \)

Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle: \( \Delta x \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} \)

Schr√∂dinger Equation: \( i\hbar \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial t} = \hat{H} \psi \)

Energy Quantization (Particle in a Box): \( E_n = \frac{n^2 h^2}{8mL^2} \)

Wave Function Normalization: \( \int |\psi(x)|^2 \, dx = 1 \)

Compton Effect: \( \lambda' - \lambda = \frac{h}{m_e c} (1 - \cos \theta) \)

Pauli Exclusion Principle: No two fermions can occupy the same quantum state.

Planck's Quantization: \( E_n = (n + \frac{1}{2})h\nu \)

6.2 Atomic Physics

Bohr's Model (Energy Levels): \( E_n = -\frac{k e^2}{2a_0 n^2} \)

Rydberg Formula: \( \frac{1}{\lambda} = R \left( \frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2} \right) \)

Energy of Photon: \( E = h\nu \)

Line Spectra: \( E = h \nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda} \)

Zeeman Effect: \( \Delta E = \pm \frac{e}{2m_e} B m_j \)

Stark Effect: \( \Delta E = \frac{e}{4 \pi \epsilon_0} \langle \psi | r | \psi \rangle E \)

6.3 Nuclear Physics

Binding Energy: \( E_B = (Z m_p + (A - Z) m_n - m_{\text{nucleus}}) c^2 \)

Radioactive Decay Law: \( N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t} \)

Half-Life: \( t_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda} \)

Decay Constant: \( \lambda = \frac{\ln 2}{t_{1/2}} \)

Alpha Decay Energy: \( E = \frac{(m_{\text{parent}} - m_{\text{daughter}} - m_{\alpha}) c^2}{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}} \)

Beta Decay Energy: \( E = \frac{(m_{\text{parent}} - m_{\text{daughter}}) c^2 - m_e c^2}{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}} \)

Fission Energy: \( E_{\text{fission}} = \Delta m c^2 \)

Fusion Energy: \( E_{\text{fusion}} = \Delta m c^2 \)

6.4 Relativity

Time Dilation: \( \Delta t = \frac{\Delta t_0}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} \)

Length Contraction: \( L = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}} \)

Mass-Energy Equivalence: \( E = mc^2 \)

Relativistic Momentum: \( p = \frac{mv}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}} \)

Relativistic Energy: \( E = \sqrt{(pc)^2 + (mc^2)^2} \)

Relativistic Doppler Effect: \( \nu' = \nu \sqrt{\frac{1 - \frac{v}{c}}{1 + \frac{v}{c}}} \)

Einstein's Field Equations: \( G_{\mu \nu} = \frac{8 \pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu \nu} \)

Schwarzschild Radius: \( r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2} \)
