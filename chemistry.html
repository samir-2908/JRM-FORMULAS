1. Physical Chemistry

1.1 Chemical Arithmetic

Dalton’s Atomic Theory: Atoms are indivisible particles that combine to form compounds.

Law of Conservation of Mass: Mass cannot be created or destroyed in a chemical reaction.

Law of Definite Proportions: A chemical compound always contains the same proportion of elements by mass.

Empirical Formula: The simplest whole-number ratio of atoms in a compound.

Molecular Formula: Shows the actual number of atoms of each element in a molecule.

Limiting Reactant: The reactant that is completely consumed in a reaction.

Avogadro's Hypothesis: Equal volumes of gases, at the same temperature and pressure, contain equal numbers of molecules.

Equivalent Mass: Mass of one equivalent of a substance, used in reactions.

Avogadro's Number: \( N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ mol}^{-1} \)

Mole Concept: \( \text{Number of Moles} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Molar Mass}} \)

Percentage Composition: \( \% \text{Element} = \frac{\text{Mass of Element in Compound}}{\text{Molar Mass of Compound}} \times 100 \)

Dilution Formula: \( M_1V_1 = M_2V_2 \)

1.2 State of Matter

Ideal Gas Law: \( PV = nRT \)

Boyle’s Law: \( P_1V_1 = P_2V_2 \) (at constant temperature)

Charles’s Law: \( \frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2} \) (at constant pressure)

Avogadro’s Law: \( \frac{V_1}{n_1} = \frac{V_2}{n_2} \) (at constant temperature and pressure)

Van der Waals Equation: \( [P + a \frac{n^2}{V^2}] [V - nb] = nRT \)

Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures: \( P_{\text{total}} = P_1 + P_2 + \dots + P_n \)

Graham's Law of Effusion: \( \frac{\text{Rate of Effusion}_1}{\text{Rate of Effusion}_2} = \sqrt{\frac{M_2}{M_1}} \)

Root Mean Square Velocity: \( u_{\text{rms}} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}} \)

Compressibility Factor: \( Z = \frac{PV}{nRT} \) (for real gases)

1.3 Atomic Structure and Periodic Classification of Elements

Planck’s Equation: \( E = h \nu \)

Bohr’s Model of the Atom: \( E_n = -\frac{13.6 \text{ eV}}{n^2} \)

Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle: \( \Delta x \Delta p \geq \frac{h}{4 \pi} \)

Schrödinger Equation: \( \hat{H} \psi = E \psi \)

Periodic Table Trends: Electronegativity increases across a period and decreases down a group.

Rydberg Formula: \( \frac{1}{\lambda} = R \left(\frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2}\right) \) (for spectral lines)

De Broglie Wavelength: \( \lambda = \frac{h}{mv} \)

Pauli Exclusion Principle: No two electrons in an atom can have the same set of four quantum numbers.

Aufbau Principle: Electrons occupy the lowest energy orbital available.

1.4 Oxidation, Reduction, and Equilibrium

Oxidation Number: The charge of an atom in a compound.

Balancing Redox Reactions: Equalize the number of electrons lost and gained.

Nernst Equation: \( E = E^0 - \frac{RT}{nF} \ln Q \)

Le Chatelier's Principle: If a dynamic equilibrium is disturbed, the system adjusts to counteract the disturbance.

Equilibrium Constant (K): \( K = \frac{[\text{Products}]}{[\text{Reactants}]} \)

Standard Electrode Potential (\( E^0 \)): \( E^0_{\text{cell}} = E^0_{\text{cathode}} - E^0_{\text{anode}} \)

Redox Titrations: \( V_1N_1 = V_2N_2 \)

Ksp (Solubility Product): \( K_{\text{sp}} = [\text{A}^m]^a \times [\text{B}^n]^b \) for a salt \( A_mB_n \) dissolving in water.

Common Ion Effect: The suppression of the degree of dissociation of a weak electrolyte by the addition of a common ion.

1.5 Volumetric Analysis

Molarity (M): \( M = \frac{\text{n}}{\text{V}} \)

Normality (N): \( N = \frac{\text{Equivalents}}{\text{Volume}} \)

Equivalent Weight: The mass of a substance that reacts with or supplies one mole of hydrogen ions.

Volume of Titrant: \( V_1C_1 = V_2C_2 \) (for acid-base titrations)

End Point Determination: The point at which the reaction is complete, usually indicated by a color change.

Indicators in Acid-Base Titrations: \( \text{Phenolphthalein: Pink (Basic), Colorless (Acidic)} \)

Gravimetric Analysis: \( \text{Mass} = \text{Equivalent Weight} \times \text{Number of Equivalents} \)

Primary Standard: A highly pure substance that serves as a reference for titrations.

1.6 Ionic Equilibrium, Acid, Base, and Salt

pH Calculation: \( \text{pH} = -\log[\text{H}^+] \)

Buffer Solutions: Resists changes in pH upon the addition of small amounts of acid or base.

Salt Hydrolysis: Reaction of a salt with water to form an acidic or basic solution.

Water Dissociation Constant (\( K_w \)): \( K_w = [\text{H}^+][\text{OH}^-] = 1 \times 10^{-14} \text{ at } 25^\circ\text{C} \)

Ka and Kb Relationship: \( K_w = K_a \times K_b \)

Hydrolysis Constant: \( K_h = \frac{K_w}{K_b} \) for a salt of a weak base and strong acid.

Degree of Ionization: \( \alpha = \frac{C_{\text{ionized}}}{C_{\text{initial}}} \)

1.7 Electrochemistry

Electrolysis: The process of driving a non-spontaneous reaction using electrical energy.

Faraday’s Laws of Electrolysis: The amount of substance produced at an electrode is proportional to the quantity of electricity passed through the electrolyte.

Electrolytic Conductance: \( \Lambda_m = \frac{\kappa}{C} \)

Kohlrausch’s Law: \( \Lambda_m^\circ = \Lambda_m^\circ(\text{cation}) + \Lambda_m^\circ(\text{anion}) \)

Electrode Potential at Non-Standard Conditions: \( E = E^0 - \frac{RT}{nF} \ln \frac{[\text{Red}]}{[\text{Ox}]} \)

Galvanic Cell Notation: \( \text{Anode} | \text{Anodic Solution} || \text{Cathodic Solution} | \text{Cathode} \)

1.8 Energetics of Chemical Reactions, Chemical Kinetics, Chemical Bonding and Shape of Molecules

Enthalpy Change (\( \Delta H \)): Heat change at constant pressure.

Gibbs Free Energy (\( \Delta G \)): \( \Delta G = \Delta H - T \Delta S \)

Activation Energy (Ea): The minimum energy required to initiate a chemical reaction.

Arrhenius Equation: \( k = A e^{-E_a/RT} \)

Heat Capacity (\( C \)): \( q = C \Delta T \)

Entropy Change (\( \Delta S \)): \( \Delta S = \frac{q_{\text{rev}}}{T} \)

Clausius-Clapeyron Equation: \( \ln \frac{P_2}{P_1} = \frac{\Delta H_{\text{vap}}}{R} \left(\frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{1}{T_2}\right) \)

Ionic Bond: Formed by the transfer of electrons between atoms, leading to the formation of cations and anions.

Hybridization: \( sp^3, sp^2, sp \) hybridization leads to tetrahedral, trigonal planar, and linear molecular geometries, respectively.

2. Inorganic Chemistry

2.1 Non-metal

Water (Molecular Weight): \( \text{Molar mass of } \text{H}_2\text{O} = 2 \times 1.008 + 15.999 = 18.015 \text{ g/mol} \)

Ozone (Molecular Weight): \( \text{Molar mass of } \text{O}_3 = 3 \times 15.999 = 47.997 \text{ g/mol} \)

Halogens (General Reaction): \( \text{X}_2 + \text{2NaCl} \rightarrow \text{2NaX} + \text{Cl}_2 \)

Nitrogen Dioxide (Molecular Weight): \( \text{Molar mass of } \text{NO}_2 = 14.007 + 2 \times 15.999 = 46.005 \text{ g/mol} \)

Hydrogen Peroxide (Molecular Weight): \( \text{Molar mass of } \text{H}_2\text{O}_2 = 2 \times 1.008 + 2 \times 15.999 = 34.016 \text{ g/mol} \)

Ozone (Reaction with Iodide): \( \text{O}_3 + \text{2I}^- + \text{2H}^+ \rightarrow \text{I}_2 + \text{O}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Ozone (Decomposition Reaction): \( \text{2O}_3 \rightarrow \text{3O}_2 \)

Nitrogen Oxides (General Reaction): \( \text{2NO + O}_2 \rightarrow \text{2NO}_2 \)

Ammonia Synthesis (Haber Process): \( \text{N}_2 + 3\text{H}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{NH}_3 \)

Sulfuric Acid Production (Contact Process): \( 2\text{SO}_2 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{SO}_3 \)

Phosphorus Pentachloride (Formation): \( \text{P}_4 + 5\text{Cl}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{PCl}_5 \)

Carbon Dioxide (Formation): \( \text{C} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{CO}_2 \)

Hydrogen Chloride (Formation): \( \text{H}_2 + \text{Cl}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{HCl} \)

2.2 Metals

Metallurgical Principle: The process of extracting metals from their ores.

Alkali Metals General Reaction: \( \text{2Na + 2H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow 2\text{NaOH} + \text{H}_2 \)

Alkaline Earth Metals General Reaction: \( \text{Ca + 2H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Ca(OH)}_2 + \text{H}_2 \)

Coinage Metals (Copper): \( \text{Cu} \text{ has a density of } 8.96 \text{ g/cm}^3 \)

Iron (Reduction of Hematite): \( \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 + 3 \text{CO} \rightarrow 2 \text{Fe} + 3 \text{CO}_2 \)

Gold (Aqua Regia Reaction): \( \text{Au} + \text{3HCl} + \text{HNO}_3 \rightarrow \text{H[AuCl}_4]^- + \text{NO} + \text{2H}_2\text{O} \)

Aluminum (Reaction with Oxygen): \( 4 \text{Al} + 3 \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2 \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3 \)

Copper (Reaction with Sulfuric Acid): \( \text{Cu} + 2 \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 \rightarrow \text{CuSO}_4 + \text{SO}_2 + 2 \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Lead (Reaction with Nitric Acid): \( \text{Pb} + 4 \text{HNO}_3 \rightarrow \text{Pb(NO}_3)_2 + \text{2NO}_2 + 2 \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Zinc (Reaction with Hydrochloric Acid): \( \text{Zn} + 2\text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{ZnCl}_2 + \text{H}_2 \)

Aluminum (Thermite Reaction): \( \text{2Al} + \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \rightarrow 2\text{Fe} + \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3 \)

Nickel (Reaction with Hydrogen): \( \text{Ni} + \text{H}_2 \rightarrow \text{NiH}_2 \)

Cobalt (Formation of Cobalt Chloride): \( \text{Co} + 2\text{Cl}_2 \rightarrow \text{CoCl}_2 \)

2.3 Extraction of Metal

Zinc Extraction (Reduction): \( \text{ZnO + C} \rightarrow \text{Zn + CO} \)

Mercury Extraction (Reduction): \( \text{HgS} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{Hg} + \text{SO}_2 \)

Iron Compound (Rusting): \( \text{4Fe + 3O}_2 \rightarrow \text{2Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \)

Aluminum Extraction (Hall-Héroult Process): \( \text{2Al}_2\text{O}_3 + 3\text{C} \rightarrow 4\text{Al} + 3\text{CO}_2 \)

Copper Extraction (Froth Floatation): \( \text{CuFeS}_2 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{Cu}_2\text{O} + \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \)

Lead Extraction (Reduction of Lead Oxide): \( \text{PbO} + \text{C} \rightarrow \text{Pb} + \text{CO} \)

Nickel Extraction (Carbonyl Process): \( \text{Ni} + \text{4CO} \rightarrow \text{Ni(CO)}_4 \)

Chromium Extraction (Reduction with Aluminum): \( \text{Cr}_2\text{O}_3 + 2 \text{Al} \rightarrow 2 \text{Cr} + \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3 \)

Silver Extraction (Cyanide Process): \( \text{2Ag} + \text{NaCN} + \text{H}_2\text{O} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{2[Ag(CN)}_2]^- + \text{2NaOH} \)

Magnesium Extraction (Pidgeon Process): \( \text{Si} + 2\text{MgO} \rightarrow 2\text{Mg} + \text{SiO}_2 \)

Iron Extraction (Blast Furnace): \( \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 + 3\text{CO} \rightarrow 2\text{Fe} + 3\text{CO}_2 \)

Cobalt Extraction (Reduction with Hydrogen): \( \text{Co}_2\text{O}_3 + 3\text{H}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{Co} + 3\text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Manganese Extraction (Reduction with Carbon): \( \text{MnO}_2 + 2\text{C} \rightarrow \text{Mn} + 2\text{CO} \)

Titanium Extraction (Kroll Process): \( \text{TiO}_2 + 2\text{Cl}_2 + 2\text{C} \rightarrow \text{TiCl}_4 + 2\text{CO} \)

3. Organic Chemistry

3.1 Introduction

General Formula for Alkanes: \( \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n+2} \)

General Formula for Alkenes: \( \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n} \)

General Formula for Alkynes: \( \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n-2} \)

General Formula for Alcohols: \( \text{R-OH} \)

General Formula for Carboxylic Acids: \( \text{R-COOH} \)

Nomenclature Rule (IUPAC): Identifying longest carbon chain, numbering, and naming substituents.

Reaction Mechanism: A sequence of steps (elementary reactions) that describe the conversion of reactants to products.

3.2 Hydrocarbons

Hydrocarbon General Formula (Aromatic): \( \text{C}_6\text{H}_6 \) (Benzene)

Alkane Combustion Reaction: \( \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n+2} + (n + \frac{1}{4}(2n + 2)) \text{O}_2 \rightarrow n \text{CO}_2 + (n + 1) \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Alkene Addition Reaction (Hydrogenation): \( \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n} + \text{H}_2 \rightarrow \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n+2} \)

Alkyne Addition Reaction (Hydrohalogenation): \( \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n-2} + \text{HX} \rightarrow \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n-1}\text{X} \)

Benzene Electrophilic Substitution: \( \text{C}_6\text{H}_6 + \text{X}_2 \rightarrow \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{X} + \text{HX} \)

Alcohol Oxidation (Primary): \( \text{R-CH}_2\text{OH} + \text{O} \rightarrow \text{R-CHO} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{R-COOH} \)

Alkene Ozonolysis: \( \text{R-CH=CH-R} + \text{O}_3 \rightarrow \text{R-CHO} + \text{R-CHO} \)

3.3 Haloalkanes and Haloarenes

Haloalkane Nucleophilic Substitution: \( \text{R-X} + \text{Nu}^- \rightarrow \text{R-Nu} + \text{X}^- \)

Haloalkane Elimination Reaction: \( \text{R-X} + \text{Base} \rightarrow \text{R=R} + \text{HX} \)

Haloarene Nucleophilic Substitution (Sandmeyer Reaction): \( \text{Ar-NH}_2 + \text{NaNO}_2 + \text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{Ar-NO}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Haloarene Reactivity: \( \text{Ar-X} + \text{NaOH} \rightarrow \text{Ar-OH} + \text{NaX} \)

Reactivity of Haloalkanes (Wurtz Reaction): \( 2 \text{R-X} + 2 \text{Na} \rightarrow \text{R-R} + 2 \text{NaX} \)

3.4 Alcohols, Phenols, and Ethers

Alcohol Dehydration (Esterification): \( \text{R-OH} + \text{R'-COOH} \rightarrow \text{R-COOR'} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Phenol Reaction with Bromine: \( \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{OH} + \text{Br}_2 \rightarrow \text{C}_6\text{H}_4(\text{Br})_3 + \text{HBr} \)

Ether Cleavage with HI: \( \text{R-O-R'} + \text{HI} \rightarrow \text{R-I} + \text{R'-I} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Phenol as an Acid: \( \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{OH} + \text{NaOH} \rightarrow \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{ONa} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Ethers (General Formula): \( \text{R-O-R'} \)

Alcohol Reaction with Acid Chloride: \( \text{R-OH} + \text{R'-COCl} \rightarrow \text{R-COOR'} + \text{HCl} \)

3.5 Aldehydes, Ketones, Carboxylic Acids, and Derivatives

Aldehyde Oxidation: \( \text{R-CHO} + \text{O} \rightarrow \text{R-COOH} \)

Ketone Reduction (Clemmensen Reduction): \( \text{R-CO-R'} + \text{Zn(Hg)} \rightarrow \text{R-CH}_2\text{-R'} \)

Carboxylic Acid Esterification: \( \text{R-COOH} + \text{R'-OH} \rightarrow \text{R-COOR'} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Carboxylic Acid Reaction with Base: \( \text{R-COOH} + \text{NaOH} \rightarrow \text{R-COONa} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Ketone Formation (Friedel-Crafts Acylation): \( \text{Ar-H} + \text{R-COCl} \rightarrow \text{Ar-CO-R} + \text{HCl} \)

Carboxylic Acid Decarboxylation: \( \text{R-COOH} \rightarrow \text{R-H} + \text{CO}_2 \)

3.6 Nitro Compounds and Amines

Nitrobenzene Reduction (Beckmann Rearrangement): \( \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NO}_2 + \text{H}_2 \rightarrow \text{C}_6\text{H}_5\text{NH}_2 \)

Amines Formation (N-alkylation): \( \text{R-NH}_2 + \text{R'-X} \rightarrow \text{R-NR'}_2 + \text{HX} \)

Amines Reaction with Acyl Chlorides: \( \text{R-NH}_2 + \text{R'-COCl} \rightarrow \text{R-NH-COR'} + \text{HCl} \)

Nitro Compounds Reactivity (Reduction): \( \text{R-NO}_2 + 3\text{H}_2 \rightarrow \text{R-NH}_2 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O} \)

Amines as Bases: \( \text{R-NH}_2 + \text{HCl} \rightarrow \text{R-NH}_3^+ \text{Cl}^- \)

Amines Reaction with Nitroalkanes (Henry Reaction): \( \text{R-NH}_2 + \text{R'-NO}_2 \rightarrow \text{R-NH-R'} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \)
